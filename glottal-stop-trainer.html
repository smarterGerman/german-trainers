The glottal stop (Knacklaut) is a brief closure of the vocal cords that occurs before vowels in German, especially at word and syllable beginnings.

With glottal stop: beachten (be-'achten), Verein (Ver-'ein), Theater (The-'ater)
Without glottal stop: Beamter, Theater (smooth), Realismus

Listen to each word and decide whether you hear a glottal stop (a brief pause/click before a vowel) or a smooth transition between syllables.
