Auslautverhartung - German final devoicing

In German, voiced consonants (d, b, g) become voiceless (t, p, k) at the end of a syllable or word.

Examples:
Hund sounds like "Hunt" (but spelled with d!)
lieb sounds like "liep" (but spelled with b!)
Tag sounds like "Tak" (but spelled with g!)

The spelling stays the same - you can see the original sound in related words:
Hund/Hunde, lieb/lieben, Tag/Tage
