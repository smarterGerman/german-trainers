k or ck?

Listening Trainer
Space Play F k J ck

Listen whether you hear a long or a short vowel before the k-sound. It's not going to be easy. So don't give up too soon. This rule also helps when reading: when you see ck, you know the vowel before is short.

ck = after SHORT vowel
backen, Zucker, Stock, Decke, Brücke

k = after LONG vowel
Haken, Musik, Pokal, Fabrik

k = after DIPHTHONG (always long: au, ei, ie)
pauken, pieksen, Streik, Mosaik

k = after CONSONANT
Markt, Werk, Park, Bank, Dank
