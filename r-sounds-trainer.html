German has two main R pronunciations:

Uvular R - The "real" R sound, produced in the throat.
Used at the beginning of syllables or after consonants.
Examples: rot, Brot, fragen, grun

Vocalic R - Sounds like a weak "a" or "uh" sound.
Used at the end of syllables, especially after vowels.
Examples: Vater, hier, Uhr, der, wer

Quick rule: Beginning/after consonant = Uvular | End of syllable = Vocalic
