Vocalic R (the open "o" sound)
Used at the end of syllables, after vowels: Vater, hier, Uhr, der, wer

It's like a very short, open "o" (IPA: /…ê/). Don't pronounce any R sound at all - the tongue stays relaxed and low. "Vater" sounds like "Fah-tah" with a very short final vowel.

Note: The rolled/trilled R (like in Spanish or Italian) is not standard High German. While some regional dialects use it, in standard German pronunciation (Hochdeutsch) the uvular R is the norm.
