PF or F?

Space Play F pf J f
Listen for the difference between PF and F.

German pf is a single sound (affricate) where you start with closed lips (like "p") and release into "f". English speakers often drop the "p" and just say "f" - this is wrong. Make sure to always pronounce the P.

Why pf vs f? It's historical - no spelling rule exists. The High German consonant shift (~1000 years ago) turned Germanic /p/ and /pp/ into /pf/:
  • pf words = old Germanic: Apfel (apple), Pfeffer (pepper), opfen (open), Pfad (path), Pfanne (pan), Pflanze (plant), Pflaume (plum)
  • f words = Latin/French loanwords or didn't shift: Fenster (Latin fenestra), Familie, Fest
Trick: If the English cognate has "p" or "pp", the German word likely has "pf".
